If you are thinking of spending some extra time while you're up here, there's quite a lot to do in the area.

Hiking

It's the Adirondacks! There's so many mountains nearby, go to the top of them! I've always wanted to hike up nearby Blue Mountain. And the High Peaks region (near Lake Placid) is two hours drive north. If mountains aren't your thing, there's tons of relatively flat trails all over the park, so finding something at your level shouldn't be very difficult.

Swimming

The Adirondacks are full of some beautiful lakes and ponds. There's public beaches in just about every town, and if you're not in a town and come upon a body of water, feel free to hop in for a dip. So bring your swimsuits and have them available for any occasion, some of us try to jump in the nearest body of water after every long hike!

Mountain towns

If walking around cute towns with an ice cream cone and going window shopping is your thing, there's many cute mountain towns nearby to visit. Most, but not all, of them are named after lakes: Blue Mountain Lake, Long Lake, Northville, Tupper Lake, Saranac Lake, Old Forge, and Lake Placid are all cute towns worth exploring.

Lake Placid

Lake placid is one of our favorite areas and is worth its own mention. It's a two hour drive north from Speculator. Once there, you can explore the site of 2 (TWO!) olympic games, which includes a bobsled run and ski jumps. Or you can explore the cute downtown and olympic museum or drive up to the top of Whiteface Mountain. Unfortunely, the infamous Pipe and Book has closed, so you'll have to bring your own pipes and books.

Amusement parks

There's Enchanted Forest Water Safari (where the fun never stops) in Old Forge, which is about an hour and a half west of Speculator. Additionally, about an hour east will take you to The Great Escape or Adirondack Extreme Adventure Course which are both near Lake George.

On the water

There's lakes everywhere! Big ones like Lake George and the Great Sacandaga Lake and little ones like Lake Pleasant and Oxbow Lake. When there's lakes, you can go out on the water. There's motor boat and jet ski rentals for the big ones, and kayak and canoe rentals in the smaller ones. A few even have sea planes that you can charter for a ride.

Mountain Biking

There's downhill mountain biking at Oak Mountain. Bring your own bike and buy a day pass so you can haul it up on the lift and then show the mountain who's boss all day long. More info here.

Every type of Golf

For the traditionalists, there's the Lake Pleasant Golf course in, unsurprisingly, the town of Lake Pleasant. For those that don't have that level of commitment, Oak Mountain offers both a new course with nine holes of mini golf and they are planning on adding a disc golf course this Spring.
