If you have thinking of spending some extra time while you're up here, there's quite a lot to do in the area.

Mountain Biking

There's downhill mountain biking at oak mountain. Bring your own bike and you can bring it up on the lift and then ride za down. More info here.

Boat rental

Lots of lakes, can rent boats and go motoring, kayaking, canoeing in the lakes

Swimming

The Adirondacks are full of some beautiful lakes and ponds. So bring Do some swimming at the beaches

Hiking

It's the Adirondacks! There's so many mountains nearby, go to the top of them! I've always wanted to hike up Blue Mountain. And the High Peaks region (near Lake Placid) two hours drive north. If mountains aren't your thing, there's tons of relatively flat trails all over the park, so finding something at your level shouldn't be very difficult.

Lake Placid

Lake placid is one of our favorite areas and is worth its own mention. It's a two hour drive north from Speculator. Once there, you can explore the site of two (2!) olympic games, which includes a bobsled run and ski jumps. You can drive to the top of Whiteface Mountain. You can explore their cute downtown and olympic museum. Unfortunely, The Pipe and Book has closed, so you'll have to bring your own pipes and books.

Mountain towns

There's so many cute mountain towns nearby — if walking around town with an ice cream cone and doing shopping is your thing, there's many options available. Most of them are named after lakes: Northville, Old Forge, Blue Mountain Lake, Long Lake, Tupper Lake, Saranac Lake, and Lake placid are all cute towns worth exploring.

Amusement parks

There's Enchanted Forest Water Safari (where the fun never stops) in Old Forge, which is about an hour and a half west. Of course The Great Escape is about an hour east, in Lake George. Also in Lake George, Adirondack Extreme Adventure Course looks pretty neat.
